# Business Recommendations — SaaS Analytics Project

## 1. Prioritize moving customers to higher-tier plans
**Finding:** Churn drops sharply as plan tier rises.
**Data:** Basic 46.5% churn → Standard 23.4% → Premium 10.1% → Enterprise 4.3%.
**Action:** Build a structured upsell motion — target Basic/Standard customers with high usage and low support tickets for a Premium/Enterprise upgrade path.
**Expected Impact:** Even a 10-point reduction in Basic-tier churn would meaningfully lower overall churn, since Basic represents 37% of the customer base.

## 2. Double down on Enterprise account acquisition
**Finding:** Enterprise customers generate the highest lifetime value by a wide margin.
**Data:** Enterprise LTV $5,576 vs Basic $269 — a 20x difference.
**Action:** Shift sales/marketing budget toward outbound enterprise sales motions rather than broad-based paid acquisition.
**Expected Impact:** Enterprise is only 11.3% of customers but contributes disproportionately to revenue — growing this segment has outsized impact.

## 3. Investigate and improve support experience for at-risk customers
**Finding:** Support ticket volume is strongly linked to lower satisfaction and higher churn.
**Data:** Customers with 8+ tickets show satisfaction dropping toward 3-4/10; churned customers average 5.53 satisfaction vs 7.14 for active.
**Action:** Flag active customers crossing a ticket threshold (5+) for proactive outreach before they churn.
**Expected Impact:** Early intervention on high-ticket accounts could meaningfully reduce preventable churn.

## 4. Marketing channel budget — no reallocation currently justified
**Finding:** Unlike plan tier, marketing channel shows almost no difference in churn or LTV.
**Data:** LTV ranges only $1,420-$1,490 across all 6 channels; churn 25.9%-27.6%.
**Action:** Optimize purely on cost-per-acquisition and volume efficiency rather than reallocating based on customer quality.
**Expected Impact:** Avoids a wrong decision — raw signup volume (e.g., from Paid Ads) doesn't indicate better customer value in this data.
